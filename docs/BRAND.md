# FlowCue — Brand Document
*The teacher's cue card. In your pocket. In the dark.*

---

## What It Is

FlowCue is a mobile app for yoga and breathwork teachers. It holds scripts, cues, and guided breathing sequences in a clean, dark-mode interface designed to be glanced at — not stared at — mid-class. Built for low-light studio environments, it gets out of your way so you can stay present with your students.

---

## Brand Essence

**One word:** *Presence*

FlowCue doesn't compete for attention. It supports the moment. Like a well-placed cue card — it's there when you need it, invisible when you don't.

---

## Audience

**Primary:** Yoga teachers (200-hour trained and above), pranayama facilitators, breathwork guides
**Secondary:** Meditation teachers, sound healers, somatic practitioners

**Context of use:** Dark or dim studios, in-motion teaching, moments between cues, backstage before a workshop

## Visual Identity

### Aesthetic Direction

**Refined sacred minimalism** — the visual equivalent of a candle-lit shala. Think Insight Timer meets a Japanese ink drawing. Line art, negative space, and one breath of color per screen.

Reference images that capture the spirit:
- The observatory illustration: dark navy ground, rose gold line drawing, stars scattered with intention. That restraint. That warmth.
- Insight Timer's animated breath circle: one thing. One focus. No noise.

### Color Palette

| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Deep Indigo** | `#1A1F2E` | Primary background |
| **Night Slate** | `#141824` | Secondary background, cards |
| **Rose Ash** | `#C4907A` | Primary accent — line art, active states |
| **Warm Pearl** | `#EDE8DF` | Primary text |
| **Muted Sage** | `#7A8C7E` | Secondary text, inactive states |
| **Ember** | `#D4845A` | Highlight, breath timers, CTAs |
| **Deep Shadow** | `#0E1019` | Overlays, bottom sheets |

No white backgrounds. No bright light. Ever.

### Typography

| Role | Font | Style |
|---|---|---|
| **Display / Headers** | Cormorant Garamond | Light or Regular, generous tracking |
| **Body / UI** | DM Sans | Regular, compact |
| **Cue scripts** | Lora | Italic for in-practice read-aloud feel |
| **Labels / Tags** | DM Sans | Uppercase, tight tracking, small size |

Typography feels like it belongs in a well-designed journal — legible in low light, never screaming.

### Illustration Style

- **Line drawings only** — single-weight strokes, no fills
- **Subject matter:** yoga poses, hands in mudra, breath circles, nature (moon, mountains, waves)
- **Color:** Rose Ash on Deep Indigo. Occasionally Warm Pearl.
- **Density:** sparse. One illustration per screen, maximum. Usually none.
- **Mood:** meditative, not athletic. Stillness over effort.

Think: the observatory image — that is the exact visual register. Sacred geometry, hand-drawn quality, warm on dark.

### Motion

- **Breath animations:** slow, looping circles that expand and contract — Insight Timer's breathing orb is the north star
- **Transitions:** fade and rise — nothing slides aggressively
- **Loading states:** a single pulsing line or dot, not a spinner
- **Haptics:** gentle, timed to breath cues (single tap on inhale marker, double on exhale)
- **Speed:** everything breathes. Nothing snaps.

## UI Principles

**One thing per screen.**
If there are two things competing for attention, one of them shouldn't be there.

**Glanceable.**
A teacher should be able to look down for one second and get what they need. Hierarchy is everything.

**Dark, always.**
No light mode. This is a classroom tool. Dark = respect for the room.

**Touch is secondary.**
Large tap targets, no tiny icons. Assume one thumb, low light, moving through a room.

**No gamification.**
No streaks, no badges, no points. This is not a consumer wellness app. It's a professional tool.

---

## Screen-Level Design Language

Each screen gets:
- A single, centered purpose
- Generous vertical breathing room (padding is a feature)
- Maximum one illustration or animation
- Text hierarchy: one large thing, one small thing, nothing in between

Navigation is minimal — bottom tab bar with 4 max items. Icons only. No labels unless tapped.

## App Sections (MVP)

| Section | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Library** | Browse saved scripts by category (pranayama, opening, savasana, etc.) |
| **Cue Now** | Full-screen read mode — single script, dark background, large text |
| **Sequences** | String scripts together into a class flow |
| **Record** | Optional: record yourself delivering a cue for playback |

## Naming and Language

- App name: **FlowCue**
- Scripts are called: **cues**
- Sequences are called: **flows**
- Categories are called: **containers** (opening, grounding, peak, closing, breath)
- Users are called: **teachers** (never "users")
